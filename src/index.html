About Me

In 2018 I decided to make a career change from outdoor and mathematics education to the technology sector. I enrolled coding bootcamp in Portland that focused on full-stack development in Python and Javascript. My first job after the coding bootcamp was as a QA Automation Engineer at Nike. Currently I am taking classes at PCC toward a certificate in Creative Coding and Immersive Technologies.

Originally I am from New Jersey but between there and here, I served four years in the US Army as a Reconnaissance Specialist (19D Cavalry Scout to be precise) in South Korea and Afghanistan and earned a degree in Mathematics from Rutgers University. I have worked in New England as an outdoor educator, wilderness emergency medical technician, wilderness therapy guide, backcountry caretaker in the White Mountains of New Hampshire and math teacher at a school for competitive skiers and snowboarders.

My other interests include juggling; I am a volunteer juggling instructor for the Portland Juggling Club, and have served as Volunteer Coordinator for the Portland Juggling Festival in 2016 - 2018 and Festival Director in 2019. I also enjoy making things by hand: clothes, hats, shoes and soap among other things. Actually, it was a shoe-making workshop that brought me out to Portland in the first place.
