About Me

I am currently working as a contract Quality Assurance Engineer with Nike in Portland, Oregon. I am recent graduate of the Full Stack Web Development Course at PDX Code Guild (April, 2018). The majority of my work experience has been in forward-facing roles in military, educational and guest-services environments with an emphasis on leadership, management and communication in rapidly changing and uncertain conditions. I possess strong technical, analytical, communication and problem-solving skills. I have a natural inclination toward computer coding, seek out challenges and embrace opportunities to explore new technologies. My primary coding languages are Python and Javascript and my coding interests are in algorithm design, optimization and efficiency and visualization of information and data.

Originally I am from New Jersey but between there and here, I served four years in the US Army as a Reconnaissance Specialist (19D Cavalry Scout to be precise) in South Korea and Afghanistan and earned a degree in Mathematics from Rutgers University. I have worked in New England as an outdoor educator, wilderness emergency medical technician, wilderness therapy guide, backcountry caretaker in the White Mountains of New Hampshire and math teacher at a school for competitive skiers and snowboarders. I have volunteered in the Amazon rain forest as an animal welfare advocate and in rural Romania as an organic farm worker (and co-organizer of the 2nd Annual Red Onion Festival of Ideciu de Jos; so much fun!) I have walked the Camino de Santiago across northern Spain, hiked the Long Trail in Vermont and once spent a month wandering around the Escalante River Valley as a student on the 28-day field survival course at the Boulder Outdoor Survival School in Boulder, Utah.

My other interests include juggling; I am a volunteer juggling instructor for the Portland Juggling Club, and have served on the planning committee and as volunteer coordinator for the Portland Juggling Festival in 2016, 2017, and 2018. I am currently a student of Improvisational Theater at the Brody Theater in Downtown Portland and have a few crafty hobbies like soap-making, leather-work and bookbinding. Actually, it was a shoe-making workshop that brought me out to Portland in the first place. I also like to snowboard.
